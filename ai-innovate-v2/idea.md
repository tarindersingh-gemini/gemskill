# AI Innovate Hackthon
Conduct AI innovate hackthon to foster creativity and problem-solving using AI technologies in gemini private ltd, with the help of github copilot and minimum people resources (mentor, judges, evaluators).

## Assumptions
- Participation count: For hackthon in industry standard, we can assume around 10 to 20 percent participants. So, for 1100 employees, we can expect around 110 to 220 participants.
- Team size: Assuming average team size of 4 members, we can expect around 27 to 55 teams.

## Resources Needed
- Mentors: 2 experienced AI professionals to guide teams and evaluate proposals.
- Judges: 2 to 10 interested members from various departments to evaluate final submissions.
- Technical Support: IT support to assist with resource allocation and technical issues.
- Prizes: 4 iPads for winning team members and toasters for runners-up.
- Marketing Materials: 1 designer for creating posters, flyers, and digital content for promotion.
- Communication Platforms: Microsoft Teams for team collaboration and communication.



## Process Flow
### Stage 1. Marketing and Announcement (Week 1 and week 2)
- Set a date and duration (e.g., 2 weeks).
- Communicate the event to all employees.
    1. Emails (weekly),
    2. Posters and flyers on TVs in office premises.
        - Create visually appealing posters highlighting key details of the hackathon (Winner's Price: Ipads).
        - Create Memes on AI themes to generate interest.
    3. Announcements in general emaployee team meetings (all-hands, department meetings, monthly-ai meetings). 

### Stage 2. Ideation (week 3)
- Encourage participants to form teams (max 4) and brainstorm ideas.
- Submit idea via github MR in specified format (github copilot instructions will be provided for proposal validation).
- Resource Requirements (e.g., data, APIs, tool, platforms) for the proposed solution

### Stage 3. Proposal Review and Approval (week 3)
- Level 1: Review via github copilot agent to handle proposals that are non-standard, duplicate or too vague.
- Level 2: Manual review of filtered proposals by mentors to select the most promising ideas.
- Approved proposals will move under "approved" directory in the repo.
- Analyze resource requirements, and arrange necessary resources for each approved proposal.
- Notify teams of approval.

### Stage 4. Development Kickoff (Week 4)
- Officially kick off the development phase for approved proposals.
- Provide access to necessary resources (e.g., datasets, APIs, platorm, development tools).
- Set up communication channels on microsoft-teams for teams to collaborate and seek help from organizers.

### Stage 5. Midway Check-in (Week 5)
- Schedule a midway check-in to monitor progress.
- Teams to submit midway deliverables via github MR.
- Provide feedback and guidance to teams.

### Stage 6. Final Submission & Evaluation (Week 6)
- Final project submission via github MR in specified format
- A validation LLM instructions will be provided for final submission validation
- Evaluation:
    - Level 1: Automated evaluation using github copilot agent to validate final submissions on evaluation criteria.
    - Level 2: Manual evaluation by 2 mentors to shortlist top 10 submissions.
    - Level 3: Manual evaluation by Interested Judges Panel (2 to 10 members) to select winners.

### Stage 7. Awards and Recognition (Week 6)
- Announce winners and award prizes.
    - First Prize: iPads for each team member of the winning team.
    - 10 Runners-up: Toasters.
- Recognize all participants with participation certificates.
- Showcase winning projects in company meetings or newsletters.
 
### Stage 8. Post-Hackathon Review (Week 10)
- Gather feedback from participants, mentors, and judges.
- Analyze the success of the hackathon against initial objectives.
- Document lessons learned and suggestions for future hackathons.
- Plan for potential implementation of winning solutions within the organization.


## Timeline Summary
- Week 1-2: Marketing and Announcement
- Week 3: Ideation and Proposal Submission
- Week 3: Proposal Review and Approval
- Week 4: Development Kickoff
- Week 5: Midway Check-in
- Week 6: Final Submission & Evaluation
- Week 6: Awards and Recognition
- Week 10: Post-Hackathon Review
