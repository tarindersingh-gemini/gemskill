# JUDGING CRITERIA

## üèÜ Evaluation Framework

Submissions will be evaluated on **5 main criteria** with a total of **100 points**.

### 1. Innovation & Creativity (25 points)

**What judges look for:**
- Originality of the solution approach
- Creative use of AI technology
- Novel problem-solving methods
- Uniqueness compared to existing solutions

**Scoring Guidelines:**
- **20-25 pts (Excellent):** Highly original, creative AI application, groundbreaking approach
- **15-19 pts (Good):** Solid innovation, interesting use of AI, some uniqueness
- **10-14 pts (Average):** Standard approach, common AI use, limited innovation
- **0-9 pts (Poor):** Copycat solution, no innovation, basic AI use

---

### 2. Technical Excellence (25 points)

**What judges look for:**
- Quality of implementation
- Appropriate use of AI technology
- Code quality and architecture
- Scalability and performance
- Technical sophistication

**Scoring Guidelines:**
- **20-25 pts (Excellent):** Robust implementation, excellent architecture, production-ready quality
- **15-19 pts (Good):** Solid implementation, good code quality, working prototype
- **10-14 pts (Average):** Basic implementation, functional but rough, technical issues
- **0-9 pts (Poor):** Buggy, poor quality, doesn't work properly

---

### 3. Business Impact & Value (25 points)

**What judges look for:**
- Clear problem-solution fit
- Quantifiable business benefits
- Potential ROI
- Scalability to other use cases
- Real-world applicability

**Scoring Guidelines:**
- **20-25 pts (Excellent):** Huge business value, clear ROI, highly scalable, immediate impact
- **15-19 pts (Good):** Good business case, measurable benefits, practical solution
- **10-14 pts (Average):** Some value, unclear ROI, limited impact
- **0-9 pts (Poor):** No clear value, impractical, no business case

---

### 4. Presentation & Communication (15 points)

**What judges look for:**
- Clarity of problem explanation
- Quality of demo video
- Professionalism of presentation
- Effective storytelling
- Visual appeal

**Scoring Guidelines:**
- **13-15 pts (Excellent):** Crystal clear, compelling story, professional quality, engaging
- **10-12 pts (Good):** Clear communication, good demo, well organized
- **7-9 pts (Average):** Understandable but rough, basic presentation
- **0-6 pts (Poor):** Confusing, poor quality, hard to understand

---

### 5. Feasibility & Implementation Potential (10 points)

**What judges look for:**
- Realistic to implement
- Technical feasibility
- Resource requirements
- Integration complexity
- Time to production

**Scoring Guidelines:**
- **8-10 pts (Excellent):** Very feasible, can go to production quickly, low complexity
- **6-7 pts (Good):** Feasible with some effort, reasonable resources needed
- **4-5 pts (Average):** Challenging but possible, significant resources needed
- **0-3 pts (Poor):** Not feasible, unrealistic, impractical

---
