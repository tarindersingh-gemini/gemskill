# GENERAL PROGRAM QUESTIONS

## üìÖ Q: What is AI Innovate Month?

**A:** AI Innovate Month is a comprehensive 5-week program combining learning and innovation. The first 2 weeks provide AI learning resources and training, Week 2 launches a hackathon where teams propose solutions to real business problems, Weeks 3-4 are for building prototypes, and Week 5 features judging and an award ceremony.

**Program Structure:**
- **Weeks 1-2:** Learning Phase (AI fundamentals, LLMs, Agents)
- **Week 2:** Hackathon Kickoff (problem statements released)
- **Weeks 3-4:** Build Phase (14 days to create solution)
- **Week 5:** Judging, Results, and Award Ceremony

---

## üìö Q: Do I need AI experience to participate?

**A:** No! AI Innovate Month is designed for **all experience levels**, from complete beginners to AI experts.

**For Beginners:**
- Weeks 1-2 provide foundational learning resources
- Beginner-friendly courses and tutorials available
- Non-technical roles can contribute to problem definition, UX, and business case
- Mentors available to guide you

**For Experienced Participants:**
- Advanced learning resources available
- Opportunity to build cutting-edge solutions
- Mentor others and share expertise

**Best Approach:** Form cross-functional teams with mixed experience levels!

## ‚è∞ Q: How much time commitment is required?

**A:** Time commitment varies by phase:

**Weeks 1-2 (Learning Phase):**
- **Recommended:** 3-5 hours/week
- **Flexible:** Self-paced learning, attend sessions of interest
- **Optional:** All learning activities are optional but encouraged

**Weeks 3-4 (Build Phase):**
- **Minimum:** 10-15 hours total (distributed across 2 weeks)

- **Flexible:** Work evenings/weekends, coordinate with team

**Week 5 (Results & Ceremony):**
- **Required:** 1-2 hours for award ceremony (if finalist)
- **Optional:** Showcase viewing

**Total Time Investment:** 20-30 hours over 5 weeks (mostly flexible timing)

- Shows imaginative thinking

**2. Technical Excellence (25%):**

- Appropriate use of AI technology

**5. Feasibility & Implementation (10%):**

- **Feasibility:** Can actually be implemented

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**
- Overscoping (trying to do too much)
- Poor demo/presentation
- No clear business case
- Ignoring feasibility
